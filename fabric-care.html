Wardrobe Reference

Fabric Care

Wash temperatures, dry clean guidance, storage rules, and stain removal for every fabric in your wardrobe. Treat the clothes properly and they last a decade.
Investment fabrics — handle with care
🧶
Cashmere
Turtlenecks · Crew necks · Cardigan
Handle with care
Wash
30°C max, delicate cycle or hand wash. Cold water preferred.
Turn inside out. Use a cashmere-specific detergent or baby shampoo.
Tumble dry
Never tumble dry. Heat destroys cashmere fibres permanently.
Lay flat on a clean towel to dry — never hang, it stretches.
Iron
Never iron directly. Steam only, from a distance.
A garment steamer is the best investment for cashmere care.
Dry clean
Dry clean is safe but not always necessary for light wear.
Dry clean once a season. Hand wash between wears if lightly worn.
🪱 Pilling
Use a cashmere comb or fabric shaver after every 3–4 wears.
Pilling is normal — removal makes it look new again.
🦋 Storage
Fold, never hang. Store with cedar balls or lavender sachets.
Wash before storing for the season — moths are attracted to body oils.
✨
Silk
Blouses · Camisole · Scarf
Handle with care
Wash
30°C max, hand wash preferred. Silk delicate cycle if machine washing.
Use a silk-specific detergent. Never wring — press gently in a towel.
Tumble dry
Never tumble dry. Heat and agitation destroy silk permanently.
Iron
Low heat only, inside out, while slightly damp.
Place a cotton cloth between iron and silk. Steam is safer than direct contact.
Dry clean
Dry clean is the safest option for structured silk blouses.
For fluid silk camisoles and scarves, careful hand washing is fine.
⚠️ Avoid
Perfume, deodorant, and sweat weaken silk fibres over time.
Apply perfume before dressing. Let it dry before silk touches skin.
🌿
Linen
Trousers · Blazer · Summer shirts
Easy care
Wash
40°C, gentle cycle. Linen is robust compared to silk and cashmere.
Wash inside out to prevent fading. Dark linens may bleed initially.
Tumble dry
Low heat tumble dry is acceptable, but may shrink slightly.
Remove slightly damp and hang to finish — prevents excessive creasing.
Iron
Linen irons beautifully at high heat while still slightly damp.
Linen creases are part of its character — not every crease needs removing.
📏 Shrink
Linen can shrink 3–5% on first wash at high temperatures.
Pre-wash before first wear if unsure of size. Always wash at 30–40°C after that.
🐑
Wool & Merino
Coat · Blazer · Merino knitwear
Handle with care
Wash
30°C wool cycle or hand wash cold. Never hot — will felt irreversibly.
Merino is more robust than cashmere and can handle gentle machine washing.
Tumble dry
Never tumble dry wool or merino. Heat causes permanent felting and shrinkage.
Iron
Medium heat with steam, inside out. Use a pressing cloth on the wool coat.
The wool coat rarely needs ironing — hang in a steamy bathroom to refresh.
Dry clean
The wool coat should be professionally dry cleaned once per season.
Merino knitwear can be hand washed — dry clean is only needed for structured wool pieces.
👖
Denim
Straight-leg jeans · Denim jacket
Easy care
Wash
30°C cold, inside out to preserve colour.
Wash less often than you think — airing between wears keeps dark denim looking new for longer.
Tumble dry
Low heat or air dry. High heat fades and shrinks denim significantly.
Hang to dry when possible — denim holds its shape better when air-dried.
💡 Tip
Wash dark indigo jeans inside out with a tablespoon of white vinegar first wash — sets the dye and prevents bleeding.
Dark denim fading is the main enemy. Cold water and infrequent washing are your best tools.
☁️
Cotton
Breton · White shirt · Shorts
Easy care
Wash
40°C for most cotton. 60°C for whites if needed.
Wash Bretons inside out — navy stripes can bleed initially.
Tumble dry
Tumble dry on medium heat. Cotton is robust.
Remove promptly — cotton creases badly if left in the drum.
Iron
Cotton irons well at high heat while damp.
White shirts iron best with a little starch spray — keeps the collar crisp all day.
📏 Shrink
Cotton shrinks at 60°C+. Wash at 40°C to maintain size.
First wash at 30°C if the item fits perfectly off the rack — plays it safe.
👜
Leather
Tote · Crossbody · Loafer · Flat mule
Never wash
Wash
Never machine wash or submerge leather. Water damages the hide and causes stiffening.
Wipe with a slightly damp cloth only. Dry immediately.
✨ Clean
Use a leather cleaner or saddle soap monthly for bags and shoes.
A soft cloth and leather conditioner (like Saphir) keeps leather supple and prevents cracking.
💧 Rain
If leather gets wet, stuff the bag with tissue paper and let dry naturally away from heat.
Apply leather protector spray at the start of each season — especially for shoes.
🌡️ Storage
Store bags stuffed with tissue, in their dust bag. Never in plastic which traps moisture.
Shoes: cedar shoe trees maintain shape and absorb moisture between wears.
🌺
Viscose & Modal
Fluid dresses · Bias-cut pieces
Handle with care
Wash
30°C max, hand wash or delicate cycle. Viscose is weakest when wet — handle gently.
Mesh laundry bag in machine protects the fabric during the cycle.
Tumble dry
Never tumble dry — viscose shrinks and distorts.
Hang to dry away from direct sunlight which fades the colour.
Iron
Low heat only, inside out while slightly damp. Steam is better than direct contact.
Viscose is the easiest fluid fabric to iron when damp — becomes difficult when fully dry.
The golden rule: when in doubt, wash cooler and slower. You can always re-wash something that isn't quite clean. You cannot un-shrink cashmere or un-felt wool.

Act immediately — every stain
The universal rule: blot, never rub. Rubbing spreads the stain and damages fibres. Cold water first — hot water sets protein stains permanently.
☕ Coffee or tea
  1. Blot immediately with a clean cloth — do not rub
  2. Flush with cold water from the back of the fabric
  3. Apply a small amount of washing-up liquid, work gently
  4. Rinse in cold water and wash as normal
⚠️ On silk: skip washing-up liquid, use cool water only and take to dry cleaner
🍷 Red wine
  1. Blot immediately — absorb as much as possible
  2. Pour cold sparkling water directly onto the stain
  3. Sprinkle salt to absorb remaining wine, leave 5 mins
  4. Brush off salt, flush with cold water, wash as normal
⚠️ On cashmere or silk: cold water blot only, take to dry cleaner
💄 Lipstick / makeup
  1. Scrape off any excess with a clean knife edge gently
  2. Apply micellar water or makeup remover to a cotton pad
  3. Blot from outside of stain toward centre
  4. Wash with gentle detergent in cold water
⚠️ On silk: micellar water only, dry clean if persists
🫒 Oil / food grease
  1. Blot off excess oil immediately — do not spread
  2. Apply cornstarch or baby powder, leave 15–30 mins
  3. Brush off powder, apply a small drop of washing-up liquid
  4. Leave 10 mins, rinse with warm water, wash as normal
⚠️ On cashmere: powder method only, then dry clean
💦 Sweat / deodorant
  1. Soak in cold water immediately — do not let dry
  2. Apply white vinegar or lemon juice to the area
  3. Leave 30 mins, then wash as normal
  4. For yellow marks: mix bicarbonate of soda with water to a paste, apply, leave 1hr
⚠️ On silk: vinegar only, very diluted. Avoid anything acidic on delicate silk
🩸 Blood
  1. Cold water only — hot water sets blood permanently
  2. Rinse under cold running water from the back of fabric
  3. Apply hydrogen peroxide (test in hidden area first)
  4. Rinse thoroughly, wash in cold water
⚠️ Never use hot water or tumble dry until stain is fully gone
🖊️ Ink / pen
  1. Place a clean cloth under the stain immediately
  2. Dab with rubbing alcohol or hand sanitiser
  3. Blot — do not rub. Work from outside in
  4. Rinse with cold water, wash as normal
⚠️ On cashmere or silk: take to dry cleaner — alcohol can damage delicate fibres
🌿 Grass / mud
  1. Let mud dry completely before treating — wet mud spreads
  2. Brush off dried mud carefully
  3. Apply washing-up liquid to the stain, work in gently
  4. Rinse with cold water, wash as normal
⚠️ On white cotton: white vinegar pre-treatment helps with grass green staining
Seasonal storage rules
Before storing anything: always wash or dry clean first. Moths and mildew are attracted to body oils, not clean fabric. Never store anything soiled.
🧶 Cashmere
  • Fold — never hang. Hanging stretches the shoulders permanently
  • Store in breathable cotton bags or pillowcases — not plastic
  • Add cedar balls or lavender sachets — replace every 6 months
  • Check for pilling before storing and remove with a cashmere comb
  • Wash or dry clean before packing away for the season
✨ Silk
  • Hang on padded hangers — not wire which distorts shoulders
  • Store away from direct light — silk fades and weakens in sunlight
  • Garment bag for structured silk blouses
  • Silk scarves: rolled or folded flat in a drawer, not compressed
  • Never store near cedar — the oils can stain light-coloured silk
🧥 Wool coat
  • Dry clean before summer storage — essential, not optional
  • Hang on a wide, padded hanger — never fold for long periods
  • Store in a breathable garment bag with cedar
  • Brush with a clothes brush after each wear to remove dust
  • Never compress — wool loses its structure permanently
👜 Leather bags
  • Stuff with acid-free tissue paper to maintain shape
  • Store in the original dust bag or a cotton pillowcase
  • Never in plastic — leather needs to breathe
  • Condition with leather cream before storing for the season
  • Store upright, not crushed under other bags
👟 Shoes
  • Cedar shoe trees in leather shoes between every wear
  • Trainers: clean before storing, stuff with tissue to hold shape
  • Leather shoes: condition before off-season storage
  • Flat mules: store on a shoe rack, not stacked
  • Never store in airtight boxes — leather needs airflow
👖 Denim
  • Hang or fold — either works for denim
  • Wash inside out, hang to dry to maintain colour
  • Dark denim: store away from direct light between wears
  • No need for special seasonal storage — denim is year-round
👗 Fluid dresses (viscose / silk)
  • Hang on padded hangers — creases fall out naturally
  • Store in garment bags away from direct light
  • Do not compress — viscose and silk crease permanently when packed tight
  • If travelling: roll gently in tissue paper rather than fold
🌿 Linen
  • Hang or fold — linen is forgiving and creases shake out
  • Store clean — linen can yellow if stored with residual oils
  • Pack away in autumn with lavender — natural moth deterrent
  • A slight crease is expected and part of linen's character
The seasonal switch checklist
Wash or dry clean everything before storing — never store worn items
Check all cashmere for pilling — treat with cashmere comb before folding away
Replace cedar balls and lavender sachets (every 6 months)
Condition leather bags and shoes before storing
Stuff leather bags with tissue paper to hold shape
Insert cedar shoe trees in all leather shoes
Check for any repairs needed — loose buttons, loose seams — before storing
Photograph the stored pieces so you remember what you have when the season returns
