Part One — Colour Analysis

Professional Colour Analysis

Your season, undertone, and the colours that work hardest for you.

Season Cool Summer — with True Summer depth
High confidence across all photos

Your skin has a clear cool, slightly pink-beige cast with no warmth or yellow. The coral top you own creates visible contrast against your skin rather than blending — a reliable undertone tell. Your hair is medium-dark cool brown, eyes are dark brown, and contrast level is medium.

You suit muted, cool, and slightly greyed versions of colours. Vivid, warm, or saturated tones work against your natural colouring. Bright white is too stark — soft white and écru are always better.

Part Two — Image & Style Analysis

Image & Style Analysis

Face shape, proportions, necklines, and silhouettes that create harmony.

Face & Features

  • Oval face — elongated and balanced, the most versatile shape
  • Similar forehead and jaw width
  • Defined brows add structure and contrast
  • Elegant neck — shown off best by turtlenecks and bateau necks

Best Silhouettes

  • Tailored and defined through shoulder and waist
  • Straight-leg trousers and jeans — high-waisted straight in rigid dark indigo is the cut that has lasted 20 years and will continue to. Avoid skinny (dated) and barrel (too trend-dependent)
  • A-line skirts and dresses
  • Midi hemlines — your frame carries them beautifully
  • Belted or self-tied styles
  • Avoid: oversized shapeless cuts, very boxy silhouettes

Hair

  • Current shoulder length is ideal — elongates without overwhelming
  • Keep colour cool-toned (ash) if you ever lighten
  • Avoid warm golden highlights — fight your undertone
  • Cool balayage in mushroom or slate tones would work well

Best Sleeve Lengths

  • Three-quarter — ideal for office
  • Full-length — elegant and polished
  • Sleeveless — fine when the cut is structured
  • Avoid cap sleeves — can broaden shoulders slightly

Necklines

Small bust means V-necks can look empty. Horizontal and rounded necklines add visual fullness and suit you far better.

Crew neck
Adds width across chest. Your turtlenecks confirm this works perfectly.
Boat / Bateau
Horizontal line creates visual fullness. Very chic for your frame.
Square neck
Structured edge adds definition without relying on décolletage.
Turtleneck
Your hero neckline. Confirmed across multiple photos. Never stop wearing these.
Gentle scoop
A soft rounded opening works well. Keep it shallow.
V-neck — avoid
Draws a vertical line that looks empty with a small bust. Skip entirely.
